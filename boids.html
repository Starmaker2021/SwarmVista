Boids Model

The Boids model, designed by Craig Reynolds in 1986, is a computational algorithm that mimics the flocking behavior of birds. The essence of this model is defined by three fundamental rules: alignment cohesion, and separation. These relatively simple rules, when applied in tandem, enable the formation of intricate patterns and coordinated movements typical for bird flocks.
Try attracting or fearing Boids by double clicking or tapping and holding the mouse in the visualization panel!
